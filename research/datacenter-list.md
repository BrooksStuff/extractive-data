## Large-Scale / Hyperscale Projects (Planned & Under Construction)

### Meta — Hyperion (Richland Parish Data Center)
- **Location:** Near Holly Ridge, Richland Parish, LA (~32.477°N, 91.632°W)
- **Developer:** Meta Platforms
- **Status:** Under construction (broke ground late 2024/early 2025)
- **Scale:** ~2,250 acres (plus an additional ~1,400 acres acquired late 2025); ~4,000,000 sq ft; initial capacity ~2 GW with a stated path to scale toward 5 GW
- **Investment:** ~$10B initial phase; $27B joint venture with Blue Owl Capital announced for expanded scope
- **Jobs:** Reported ~500 permanent jobs, ~5,000 construction jobs at peak
- **Power:** Entergy Louisiana building dedicated gas-fired generation (Franklin Farms Power Station, Holly Ridge) plus additional plants across Richland, Pointe Coupee, and St. Charles Parishes to serve the site
- **Codename:** Originally "Project Sucre"

### Hut 8 — River Bend Campus
- **Location:** West Feliciana Parish, LA
- **Developer:** Hut 8 (energy infrastructure platform); EPCM by Jacobs
- **Status:** Under construction (announced Dec 2025); Phase 1 operations targeted Q2 2027
- **Scale:** Phase 1 = two 450,000 sq ft data centers; potential to scale to ~1,000 MW utility capacity over time
- **Investment:** Up to $10B (Phase 1)
- **Jobs:** ~1,000 construction workers at peak; at least 75 direct jobs once operational (LED estimates 268 total direct + indirect)
- **Tenants/Backers:** Fluidstack is the named tenant, with a financial backstop from Google covering lease obligations; Hut 8 also announced a partnership with Anthropic (Dec 2025) tied to AI data center infrastructure (2.29 GW)
- **Sources:**

### Amazon / STACK Infrastructure — Multi-Campus Project
- **Location:** Caddo Parish and Bossier Parish, LA (interconnected sites; one near the intersection of Blanchard Latex Road and State Line Road, another on the west side of Highway 3 north of Benton)
- **Developer:** STACK Infrastructure (developer/owner); Amazon (anchor tenant/investor)
- **Status:** Announced Feb 2026; construction expected to begin within weeks of announcement
- **Investment:** $12 billion
- **Jobs:** 540 direct new jobs estimated; LED estimates ~1,700 additional indirect jobs; up to 1,500 construction jobs
- **Power:** Southwestern Electric Power Company (SWEPCO) partnering with Amazon; Amazon has committed to funding 100% of required energy infrastructure/grid upgrade costs
- **Community investment:** $400M for local water infrastructure; $250,000 community fund for STEM education
- **Notes:** Amazon's first data center campus footprint in Louisiana
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.constructiondive.com/news/amazon-louisiana-data-center-construction/813021/
  - https://constructionreviewonline.com/amazon-commits-12-billion-to-louisiana-in-landmark-data-center-expansion/

### Applied Digital — Delta Forge 1 ("Project Lightning")
- **Location:** Rapides Parish, LA — 672-acre parcel along I-49 near Boyce
- **Developer:** Applied Digital Corporation (Dallas-based)
- **Status:** Site work underway; land purchased December 2025 for $26.9M
- **Jobs:** ~1,500 construction-related jobs anticipated; ~200 permanent positions in first year; average annual wage ~$65,000
- **Local mechanism:** England District Subdistrict No. 4 created (Feb 2026) by the England Authority (Rapides Parish economic development district) to issue bonds and negotiate PILOT agreements
- **Sources:**
  - https://rapidesparishjournal.com/2026/03/30/louisianas-data-center-boom-reaches-new-heights-and-rapides-parish-is-positioned-to-compete/

## Existing / Operating Facilities (Smaller Scale, Colocation)

### EdgeConneX — SLI01 ("New Orleans" Edge Data Center)
- **Location:** 2070 Gause Blvd E, Slidell, LA (technically in the Slidell/New Orleans metro, marketed as serving New Orleans)
- **Developer/Operator:** EdgeConneX
- **Status:** Operating
- **Scale:** ~27,270 sq ft; ~1 MW of leasable power
- **Purpose:** Edge colocation facility for low-latency content delivery to the New Orleans market; carrier-neutral with AT&T, Uniti Fiber, and Cox Enterprises fiber access

## Regulatory & Policy Context Worth Knowing

- **Act 730 (2024 Regular Legislative Session):** Establishes Louisiana's state and local sales/use tax rebate on qualifying data center equipment purchases or leases — the primary state incentive mechanism cited across the Meta, Hut 8, and Amazon deals.
- **Fast-track power plant approval:** Louisiana created an expedited process in December 2025 specifically to approve new power plants built to serve data centers and other large electricity users.
  - Source: Wesley Muller, "Louisiana creates fast track to approve power plants for data centers, big electricity users," *Louisiana Illuminator*, Dec 19, 2025 — https://lailluminator.com/2025/12/19/electricity-data-centers
- **Ratepayer/financing scrutiny:** Earthjustice (on behalf of the Alliance for Affordable Energy and Union of Concerned Scientists) has formally petitioned the Louisiana Public Service Commission to investigate Meta's financing structure for the gas plants backing Hyperion, citing risk that ratepayers could be left covering costs if Meta exits the deal early. The LPSC reportedly declined to open an investigation as of late February 2026 (per The Advocate, cited in the All4Energy report below).
- **New Orleans data center moratorium:** The New Orleans City Council approved a one-year moratorium on new data centers in the city following a proposed project in New Orleans East — relevant context for why hyperscale activity is concentrated in rural/exurban parishes rather than New Orleans proper.
  - Source: https://baxtel.com/data-center/edgeconnex-new-orleans (news roundup mentioning the moratorium)
- **Independent financing/energy analysis:** All4Energy published a detailed 2026 report cross-referencing parish assessor data, Preqin fund/bondholder data, and public filings for Louisiana's major data center projects — useful as a methodology reference and likely the most rigorous independent secondary source currently available.
  - https://www.all4energy.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/2026-05-18-Data-Centers-Financing-Energy-Overview-Report.pdf
